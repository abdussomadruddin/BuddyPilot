- Money

- Fear

- Strategy

- Common mistake
- Unexpected insight
- Pattern interrupt

Score every angle privately from 1 to 10 based on:
- Scroll stopping ability
- Curiosity
- Emotional impact
- CTR potential
- Relevance to the landing page
- Buying intent

Choose only the highest scoring angle.
Do not show the brainstorming process or scoring to Abdussomad.

Every new post must use a different advertising angle, visual concept, headline, and layout from previous drafts.
Every new post must also use a genuinely different photo background. Do not reuse or closely resemble previous backgrounds.
Before generating a background, check previous draft manifests and assets. Change the location, subject, business niche, time of day, lighting, props, emotional situation, camera distance, and composition.

- Product name.

Do not display price on the poster. Price may be kept in metadata or mentioned in the caption only when useful, but the poster itself should stay clean and curiosity-driven.

Use only information from the landing page or the verified facts above.

## Poster Design

Canvas:
- 1080 x 1080 pixels.
- Square 1:1.
- Safe margin: 80px.

Layout:
- Top: large headline.
- Middle: strong visual based on the chosen angle.
- Bottom: product name, real offer, CTA.

Visual style:
- High-CTR direct response.
- Candid iPhone real-scenario background with visible human emotion.
- Looks like a real moment captured on phone camera, not a generic stock photo.
- Bold poster treatment on top: large distressed/condensed headline, strong red/white/teal contrast, punchy CTA, mobile-first.
- Performance marketing style, inspired by aggressive Meta Ads creative testing.
- Readable within 2 seconds.
- No unnecessary decoration.
- The poster must not look like a normal course advertisement. It should feel like an interesting Facebook post that accidentally sells the product.

Visual rules:
- Maximum three attention points only: huge headline, one strong emotional visual, and CTA.
- Every visual element must support the chosen advertising angle.
- The background should show a believable emotional situation: business owner stressed, confused, tired, relieved, reviewing phone/laptop, WhatsApp leads, TikTok Ads planning, follow-up work, or sales journey.
- The background must not be the same or seakan sama as recent posts. Avoid repeating the same night desk, laptop, phone, tired-owner setup. Rotate scenarios such as shop counter, home kitchen, packing orders, filming product content, small cafe, boutique rack, market stall, stock room, courier parcels, notebook planning, or customer follow-up moment.
- The candid photo must have no text, no fake dashboard, no fake chat screenshots, no readable UI, no logo, and no watermark. Add exact poster text manually after generation.
- Use only one visual concept per poster. Do not combine multiple diagrams, comparison boxes, icons, product mockups, and emotional scenes in the same creative.
- Delete every element that does not directly increase scroll-stopping power, curiosity, clarity, or CTA intent.
- If the angle is about a mistake, show the mistake visually.
- If the angle is about a strategy, show the strategy visually.
- If the angle is about a hidden problem, show the hidden problem visually.
- No fake screenshots.
- No fake testimonials.
- No fake charts.
- No fake results.
- No stock poster feeling.

Design rules:
- Maximum 2 font families.
- Large bold typography.
- Strong visual hierarchy.
- High readability.
- Keep all important text and CTA inside the 80px safe margin.
- Give every text block proper padding and a dark/contrast background when it sits on a photo.
- At 20% zoom, the headline and CTA must still be readable.
- After the first version, self-critique and redesign until the final output is at least version 5 quality. Do not send a first draft when major spacing, padding, clutter, or readability issues remain.
- Premium product mockup only if it does not invent product details.
- Do not copy a reference poster 1:1; use the reference only for intensity, hierarchy, color contrast, and direct-response energy.
